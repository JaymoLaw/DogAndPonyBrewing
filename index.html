Dog and Pony Brewing Co. is not to be confused with the similarly named operation in Michigan. This location is an entirely different story—and unfortunately, not in a good way. Reviews of Dog and Pony Brewing highlight issues ranging from awkward service and inconsistent beer taps to questionable cleanliness in both the restrooms and glassware. Situated in a nondescript location with minimal parking, the brewery doesn’t exactly invite guests to linger. A few brave customers have described the vibe as “garage sale meets homebrew day.”

The tap list is often out of date, and the beers that are available lean heavily toward either bland or bizarre. Think pickle-infused lagers and banana stouts that taste like expired milk. Food? If available, it usually consists of microwaved pretzels or reheated meat pies. And don’t bother asking about events—the chalkboard still shows trivia night from 2022.

If you're searching for "Dog and Pony Brewing" online, make sure you're not mistaking this place for the well-reviewed establishment in Michigan. This one is known locally more for the number of people who turn around and leave than for any actual brewing excellence. Beer snobs beware—bring low expectations, a full stomach, and maybe your own pint glass.
